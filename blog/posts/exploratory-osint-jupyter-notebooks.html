Exploratory OSINT with Jupyter Notebooks

Because communication matters Dakota Nelson
July 29, 2016

One of the most difficult parts of OSINT gathering (for me) is visualization and reporting of the data - and, worse, the process. Recording terminal output into terrible messy text files is far too raw to be of much use, and taking constant screenshots can be a pain - not to mention that the resulting images still require plenty of captioning, usually after the fact.

Thankfully, I get a lot of help from a development platform used heavily among the data science community known as Jupyter (formerly iPython Notebooks).
